Enhancing Urban Development Through Slum Upgrading: Lessons from Makoko

Community led design can expand access to education in flood prone settlements when paired with durable materials, maintenance plans and local ownership.

Key ideas

  • Co design with residents improves adoption and protects dignity.
  • Frugal innovation must be matched with long term maintenance.
  • Education access is a foundation for mobility and opportunity.

Why this matters

People should be able to participate fully in society, free from prejudice, with real opportunities to learn and to choose their path. Better governance and design make that possible.
